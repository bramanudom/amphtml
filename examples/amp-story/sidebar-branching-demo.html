Pet's Best Boba List

Bubble tea, or Boba, is taking the world by storm. The drink originates from Taiwan, but has evolved since its iced milk days. The various experiences of those a part of the Asian Diaspora has influenced a cornucopia of new flavors and textures.

Click on what you think the best flavor is and see what Pet thinks!

Jasmine Milk

Yes! You got it.

Jasmine milk tea, often made with green tea, is a refreshing alternative to the classic milk tea flavor. Try it iced on a summer day and hot on a winter night. Try fruit jellies in lieu of boba for a fruity delight!

This is Pet's all-time-favourite. With a lightly floral taste, she thinks jasmine milk tea is the ideal refreshing drink of choice!!

Taro (Ube)

Nice try, but no!

Taro, or Ube, is a root vegetable. With its distinctive purple color and subtle taste, taro boba can be a very divisive flavor.

Pet thinks Taro boba is delicious but is wary about the usage of heavily processed Taro powder. Pet recommends new boba go-ers to start off with real tea drinks first!.

Classic milk tea

Nice try, but no!

The classic milk tea, often sweetened with honey or with honey boba is a classic go to. Boba experts say this is the drink that will establish a boba shop's credibility. While Pet thinks milk tea is a great safe best, milk teas often lean too sweet.

When Pet is feeling a black tea, she usually goes with a cream for a better balance of sweetness!

Cheese Tea

Nice try, but no!

Cheese tea is the newest contender in the boba world. Cheese tea is often a green or black tea (sometimes fruity) with a layer of salty cream cheese foam at the very top. When mixed together, you're able to taste this slightly sweet, slight salty, and very delicious concoction.

Pet thinks this is a great choice for the adventurous people, but not boba's best.
