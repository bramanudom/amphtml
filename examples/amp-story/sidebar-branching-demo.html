What's the best bubble tea flavor?

Bubble tea, or Boba, is taking the world by storm. Sources say the drink originated in Taiwan, but the drink has evolved since its iced milk days. The various experiences of those a part of the Asian Diaspora has brought with it a cornucopia of new flavors and experiences. Find your new favourite today!

Classic milk tea

The classic milk tea, often sweetened with honey or with honey boba is a classic go to. Boba experts say this is the drink that will establish a boba shop's credibility.

Taro (Ube)

Taro, or Ube, is a root vegetable. With its distinctive purple color and subtle taste, taro boba can be a very divisive flavor.

Jasmine Milk

Jasmine milk tea, often made with green tea, is a refreshing alternative to the classic milk tea flavor. Try it iced on a summer day and hot on a winter night. Try fruit jellies in lieu of boba for a fruity delight!

Cheese Tea

Cheese tea is the newest contender in the boba world. Cheese tea is often a green or black tea (sometimes fruity) with a layer of salty cream cheese foam at the very top. When mixed together, you're able to taste this slightly sweet, slight salty, and very delicious concoction.
